Dataset and Distribution Analysis in Cybersecurity

Subject: Cyber Security — Author: Salvatore Salis — Date: 14/10/2025

Cybersecurity produces large amounts of data (logs, alerts, traffic). Statistics allow us to turn these signals into operational knowledge. This page presents two concise and educational applications:

  1. Part 1 — Descriptive analysis of a small event dataset (univariate and bivariate distributions).
  2. Part 2 — Analysis of the letter frequency distribution and decryption of a text encrypted with the Caesar cipher.

Part 1 — Security Event Dataset and Distributions (N = 41)

Part 2 — Letter Distribution & Caesar Cipher (offline)

In the Caesar cipher, each letter is shifted by a fixed offset. If the shift is unknown, it can be estimated through the letter frequency distribution: the most frequent letter in the ciphertext usually corresponds to the most frequent letter in the plaintext (or to a known count match).

Reproducibility

The local script (cesare_simple.js) and generated files are stored in the folder:

/cesare

Example execution: node cesare_simple.js --in input.txt --shift 7

Simple logic used: if a letter appears N times in the plaintext (e.g. “E” = 32) and there is a letter in the ciphertext with the same count, it is likely the shifted “E”. The shift can be found by the difference between their alphabet indices. When no known counts are available, the most frequent letter in the ciphertext is aligned to a target letter (often “E” in English).

Note: in short or non-linguistic texts (many numbers, repeated names) this assumption may fail. In longer texts, frequency analysis works very well.
